ABOUT DPSIMUN 2018

CRAS ES NOSTER

It gives us immense pleasure to play host to the third edition of  Delhi Public School Indirapuram Model United Nations, after its  successful launch in the year 2016. Like the previous editions of DPSI MUN, this conference would continue to be a platform to fashion opinions, to ignite ideas and promote international peace and understanding. We believe that  wars, no matter what reason they are fought for, are the greatest  threat to the present-day human race. Violence acts as a precursor to more conflicts, rather than solving existing ones. If at all something can put an end to it, it is the change in the mindset of  people, especially our youth, and what can be a better way to do it than by stimulating and empowering the young! With this thought in mind, we embark upon the third edition of DPSI MUN. With young diplomats debating and deliberating various issues of vital importance, we are sure it will be a great learning experience to cherish forever.

ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE- Goon or Boon?

The question isn't whether will it work. The question is, if it does, how important will it be?

This year, arguably the world's greatest living scientific mind, Stephen Hawking, and its leading techno-industrialist, Elon Musk, voiced their fears about the potentially lethal rise of artificial intelligence. They were joined by philosophers, physicists and computer scientists, all of whom spoke out about the serious risks posed by the development of greater-than-human machine intelligence.

In a widely cited op-ed co-written with MIT physicist Max Tegmark, Nobel laureate Frank Wilczek and computer scientist Stuart Russell, Hawking sounded the AI alarm. "One can imagine (AI) outsmarting financial markets, out-inventing human researchers, out-manipulating human leaders, and developing weapons we cannot even understand. Whereas the short-term impact of AI depends on who controls it, the long-term impact depends on whether it can be controlled at all."
