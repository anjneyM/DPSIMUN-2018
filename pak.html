The Secretariat

Greetings, It is our pleasure to cordially invite you to the third edition of Delhi Public School Indirapuram Model United Nations. Ever since the dawn of time, the progress of humanity has been confronted with obstacles which themselves like their solutions are ever evolving. Leaders worldwide are beginning to acknowledge the need for perceptive imagination and novelty in the way of resolving these impediments which is why the 'voice of youth' has become a force to reckon with. The young ignited minds that understand that the world they live in today, will be theirs to lead tomorrow, also understand the great jeopardy besetting global peace. Their political and diplomatic adroitness is continually challenged with every new unforseen confrontation and therefore causes them to find answers to questions left unanswered. We here at DPSI maintain that no idea that stems from such kindled minds should be left unheard. Ergo, these two days shall be abound with opportunities for all our delegates to not only put forward their idea of restoring harmony worldwide but also to contest for its ascendancy.
Since its outset, DPSI has strived to offer its delegates an unrivalled Model United Nations experience by running highly engaging and stimulating committees. Carrying forward its endeavour this year we introduce a new flagship committee, The Assembly of European States, to challenge the penchant of intellectual expertise of our MUN enthusiasts.
So pull up your socks and clear the decks for an MUN experience unlike any other you've ever had!

THE COMMITTEES

1. UNGA

United Nations General Assembly

"Diplomacy is the art of telling people to go to hell in such a way that they ask for directions."
-Winston Churchill
The United Nations General Assembly continues to be the chief representative and deliberative policy-making organ of the United Nations. Abiding by its 'One Member, One Vote' convention, it is a forum that urges its nations to strengthen multilateral relations and in the course of their dialogue, prioritize preventing the conflicts of tomorrow. Please note that the UNGA is a Double Delegation​ committee.

Agenda

Comprehensive review of the United Nations Peacekeeping Forces

2. UNHRC

United Nations Human Rights Council


"For to be free is not merely to cast off one's chains, but to live in a way that respects and enhances the freedom of others"
- Nelson Mandela.
The United Nations Human Rights Council responds to mankind's livid cry for the prevention of violation of its rights. It helps avoid the likelihood of the unrelenting wave of human rights violation which may lead the society to a perpetual state of paralysis. It aims at instilling a sense of security in all global citizens, by not only taking actions against any infringement of rights, but also concurrently spreading awareness among the destitute section of the society.

Agenda

Promotion and Preservation of Human Rights and fundamental freedom while countering terrorism

3. UNSC

United Nations Security Council


“Whether the borders that divide us are picket fences or national boundaries, we are all neighbors in a global community.”
-Jimmy Carter
The Security Council is the federation that is charged with the responsibility of maintaining peace throughout the world. Comprising of 15 members, this is a forum which witnesses deliberations, actions as well as reflections upon threats to global tranquility. It is here in the chambers of Security Council that binding verdicts are discussed and declared, and more often than not, these verdicts have been directing the ideals of world peace to actualization.

Agenda

Situation in Venezuela

4. AISM

All India Stakeholders Meet


““Some men change their party for the sake of their principles; others their principles for the sake of their party”
-Winston Churchill
The need to discuss the issues that concern the Indian population requires an unbiased panel, free from political dominance and ideological influence. The dream of overcoming barriers that obstruct our path to harmonious living cannot be realized just by groups of political leaders. What we require is a mélange of stances from equally diverse range of platforms. What we require are influential people from all walks of life to help cultivate a wider view of situations. What we need is a body that prioritizes the public’s interest over political gains. The All India Stakeholders’ Meet is just that. It is a simulation that is pragmatic yet considerate, contrasting yet united. It is, indeed, the body of the people.

Agenda

Deliberations on strengthening centre-state relations

5. AES

The Assembly of European States


Grab a shovel.
Let us unearth what remains of bones of 40 Million.
We implore you: smell the smoke of pistols, drown in the cries of famished, and weigh the worth of each deceased newborn soldier’s life.
Try to ungag yourself, as an extraordinary wave of acrimony approaches. Most of all: stay cautious, Ladies and Gentlemen, as our friends deceit, disloyalty and treachery grin from afar.
Welcome to the Great War.
We grant you, World War One was an inexcusable tragedy, yet, an unrequired one. Before the first chants of the war echoed, the string of events leading to its birth could have been snapped by but a moment of prudence and goodwill.
And, we owe you that moment.
DPSI MUN 2018 sets out to explore an age old question in the greatest simulation ever: in times when sombreness prevails, is talking the difference between raising guns or glasses?
In this historic multilateral conclave, european nations meet to orchestrate the fate of generations to come. Set shortly after the assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand, the purpose of the meet is to witness self-interest sharing the stage with benevolence, for you are armed with nothing but your diplomatic prowess. We unveil to you the battle, of not fighting The War.
